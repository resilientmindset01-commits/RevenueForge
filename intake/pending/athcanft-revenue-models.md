# Revenue-model seeds from @athcanft (indie-app monetization)

Source: Will (@athcanft), read 2026-06-20. One article read in full (the
near-zero-CPM pipeline); the rest are titles, to deepen on request. ALL revenue
numbers are the creator's UNVERIFIED marketing claims (and he sells tools, so the
incentive is to overstate). Each card is scored for FIT against the operator's
actual portfolio (PrepBrix NEET/JEE education; the forge IP; ng-monitor; tamiliq).

The honest headline: this author's models are INDIE-APP monetization (ship a
small iOS app, drive installs with cheap content/ads). That is a different
business than PrepBrix. Most are OFF-PORTFOLIO. The transferable parts are the
DISTRIBUTION engine (already taken to ../youtube-growth) and a couple of mechanics
worth noting -- not the "ship throwaway apps" model itself.

## Card 1 -- Near-zero-CPM organic distribution (READ IN FULL)
Model: flood YT/TT/IG with proven-hook clips + your CTA, near-zero marginal cost.
Channel/cost: organic short-form; cost is tooling only (his paid "Clip Factory").
Requires: a niche with viral Shorts to mimic; batch scrape+stitch+schedule.
Risk: HIGH -- mass-scraping others' clips is an IP/ToS/brand risk; unsafe for an
education brand wholesale.
FIT: PARTIAL, distribution only. The safe, ownable version (own clips in proven
formats, batch-scheduled) is captured in youtube-growth/playbook/04. NOT a
RevenueForge model on its own -- it is a distribution tactic.

## Card 2 -- iOS app -> $10K MRR ("the lazy way") + TikTok/Meta paid ads
Model: ship a small iOS app (subscription), buy installs via TikTok/Meta ads at a
CPM that yields positive LTV:CAC; the "$30K on TikTok ads" + "profitable tiktok
ads for my apps" + "2x ROAS with AI TikTok ads" articles are the ad playbook.
Requires: an app, an app-store subscription, ad budget + creative testing skill.
Risk: capital at risk (paid ads), platform dependency, churn.
FIT: OFF-PORTFOLIO. PrepBrix is not a throwaway subscription app; its revenue is
course/subscription tied to graded outcomes. The ONE transferable mechanic: his
ad-creative rule "don't invent concepts, copy what already works" matches the
Faraday format rule -- if PrepBrix ever runs paid ads, test proven creative
formats. Read-on-request if paid acquisition becomes relevant.

## Card 3 -- "Make your first $1,000 with AI models" / "Lazymaxxing slideshows"
Model: low-effort content/products generated with AI (AI image/model content;
TikTok slideshows at "600/month for $2") monetized via affiliate/app installs.
Risk: low quality / brand-dilutive; race-to-the-bottom; thin moat.
FIT: OFF-PORTFOLIO and brand-inappropriate for an education product. REJECT for
PrepBrix. Noted only so the lane records it was considered.

## Card 4 -- "Why Smart People Stay Broke" (mindset essay)
Not a model -- an opinion/mindset piece. REJECT.

## Net for the portfolio
