# RevenueForge

A catalog of REVENUE MODELS -- the different ways money can be made -- scored for
whether they fit the operator's portfolio. The "multiple revenue ways" lane.

Status: ACTIVE (confirmed 2026-06-20 as its own lane). It is the 7th author-intake
destination, distinct from VentureForge: RevenueForge is the portfolio-wide MENU
of revenue models; VentureForge EXECUTES one model as a play for a specific
venture. A model scored as a real fit graduates into a VentureForge play.

## Why it is its own lane (the boundary)
- VentureForge = business PLAYS for ONE venture (PrepBrix NEET/JEE): a specific
  move, an activation plan, a recorded outcome.
- youtube-growth = DISTRIBUTION tactics (how to get the right viewers to a video).
- RevenueForge = REVENUE MODELS, portfolio-wide: HOW money is made (ad arbitrage,
  app MRR, subscription, paid content, AI-service income, affiliate, ...), each
  scored for fit across the assets the operator already has (PrepBrix, the forge
  IP, ng-monitor, tamiliq). A model that fits becomes a VentureForge play for the
  venture it fits; RevenueForge is the menu, VentureForge executes one item.

If that VentureForge boundary feels redundant to you, RevenueForge should fold
INTO VentureForge as a "revenue-models" section instead of a separate repo. That
is your call -- this v0 exists so there is something concrete to react to.

## What a RevenueForge entry is
A model card (intake/pending/<slug>.md), each with: the model in one line, the
channel + what it costs to run, what it requires (asset, audience, capital), the
UNVERIFIED source claims tagged as such, the RISK flags (IP, ToS, brand,
regulatory), and a FIT score against the operator's actual assets -- most
honestly, "does this fit PrepBrix / the forge IP, or is it off-portfolio?"

## Layout
- intake/pending/ -- model cards awaiting scoring. First: athcanft-revenue-models.md.
- (later) models/done/ -- models scored as a real fit, promoted to a VentureForge
  play for the venture they fit.

## The discipline (same as the other forges)
- Source claims are UNVERIFIED -- a creator's revenue numbers are marketing, not
  facts. A model is a hypothesis to test, not a promise.
- "Does not fit" is a first-class, honest outcome -- most internet revenue models
  are off-portfolio for an education brand, and saying so saves attention.
- No model that risks the PrepBrix brand or breaks a platform's ToS gets promoted,
  however cheap it looks.
- ASCII, "--" not em-dash, no banned words.
